# Design Context — Noah Frank Personal Website

## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters, VCs, and founders — typically arriving via a LinkedIn link or CV footer. They spend 10–30 seconds deciding whether to keep reading. They are busy, skeptical of generic portfolios, and respond to signals of seriousness and maturity. The job to be done: form an immediate impression of Noah as someone worth reaching out to.

### Brand Personality
**Reif · Geerdet · Weitblickend** (Mature · Grounded · Forward-looking)

The site should feel like it was made by someone who knows who they are — not someone trying to impress. Confidence without arrogance. Ambition without noise.

Emotional goal: The visitor leaves feeling **impressed, curious, and trusting** — simultaneously.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial premium** — think high-end magazine meets serious professional profile.

References: Anthropic, OpenAI, Apple — specifically their restraint, whitespace, and typographic confidence.

Anti-references (explicitly avoid):
- Student portfolio vibes — cheap, template-like, generic
- LinkedIn aesthetic — bullet-listed, dry, corporate
- Tech-bro startup — too hip, buzzword-heavy, performative
- Vibe-coded / overbuilt — excessive animations, decorative noise, try-hard interactions

**Theme**: Light mode only. Warm cream background (#FAF8F5), deep warm-black text (#1A1614), terracotta accent (#C8522A) used sparingly.

**Typography**: 
- Display / headlines: **Cormorant Garamond** — elegant serif, editorial, luxury feel. Signals maturity.
- Body / UI: **Geist** (variable, self-hosted) — clean, modern, legible.
- This pairing creates the contrast between editorial prestige (name/headlines) and modern clarity (copy/nav).

### Design Principles

1. **Typography is the design.** The name in Cormorant Garamond, large and confident, IS the hero. Do not decorate around it — let it breathe.

2. **Restraint over decoration.** Every element must earn its place. No card just because it looks "rich". No animation just because it's possible. Ask: does this add meaning or just noise?

3. **Generous whitespace signals confidence.** Tight layouts feel cheap. Empty space is not wasted — it communicates that the content is secure enough to stand alone.

4. **One accent, used once.** The terracotta (#C8522A) appears in at most one place per section — a single underline, a single word, a single stroke. Never as a fill or background.

5. **Copy carries weight.** No generic phrases. Every sentence on this site should be something only Noah Frank could say. The design amplifies good copy — it cannot replace it.

6. **Interactions are quiet.** Subtle entrance animations on load (fade + translate, once only). Parallax on hero scroll. Nothing on hover beyond color shifts. No bouncing, no floating, no looping.
